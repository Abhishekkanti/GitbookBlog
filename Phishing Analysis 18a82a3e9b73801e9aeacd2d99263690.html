Phishing Analysis

Phishing is the act of attempting to obtain sensitive information from individuals by using social engineering tactics over various communication platforms such as email, SMS, phone calls, and the Internet.

  • Impersonation: Posing as legitimate organizations or individuals
  • Stealing sensitive information: Passwords, credit card numbers, sensitive files
  • Deliver and install malware: Via attachments, embedded files, or URLs
  • Exploiting humans: Preys on emotions, Human psychology.

How Does Phishing Work?

  • Authority
    • Spoofing executives, managers, IT staff
  • Trust
    • Spoofing customers, banks, partners.
  • Intimidation
    • Instill a sense of fear of consequences.
  • Social Proof
    • Validate legitimacy through consensus
  • Urgency
    • "Act NOW!"
  • Scarcity
    • Instill a fear of missing out
  • Familiarity
    • Establish credibility through recognition

Email Fundamentals

Mail Agents

  • Mail Transfer Agent (MTA)
    • Route and transfer email messages across mail servers
    • Determine appropriate route and relays
  • Mail User Agent (MUA)
    • Compose, send, receive, and manage email messages
    • Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo, Thunderbird
  • Mail Delivery Agent (MDA)
    • Accepting incoming email messages from MTAs
    • Placing the email in the recipient's inbox

Phishing Attack Types

  1. Information Gathering
    • Collecting data through reconnaissance
    • Verify existing accounts, craft credible phishes
  1. Credential Harvesting
    • Obtain login credentials from victims
    • Fake login pages, deceptive URLs
  1. Malware Delivery
    • Malicious attachments or links
    • Drive-by downloads (Automatic Downloadable content on visit)
  1. Spear Phishing
    • Targeted and customized phishing
    • Research specific individuals or organizations
  1. Whaling
    • Targeting high-profile individuals (CEOs, executives).
  1. Vishing, SMiShing, and Quishing
    • Attempts to obtain information over the phone
    • SMS messages containing malicious URLs
    • QR codes leading to malicious URLs
  1. Business Email Compromise (BEC)
    • Compromising legitimate email accounts
    • Unauthorized wire transfers, invoice scams.
  1. Spam
    • Unsolicited, irrelevant, and unwanted email
    • Not typically with malicious intent

Phishing Attack Techniques

  1. Pretexting
    1. Fabricating a backstory (Some kind of Story to trick)
    1. Manipulation under false pretense.
  1. Spoofing and Impersonation
    1. Email Address Spoofing: change email headers
    1. Domain Spoofing: spoof real site with malicious once.
  1. URL Manipulation
    1. URL Shortening: Shorten malicious links
    1. Subdomain Spoofing: Use similar links such as facebook.malicioussite.com
    1. Homograph Attacks: Replacing characters with similar once in links i ⇒ Î
    1. Typosquatting: Using similar spelling as of the real one LinkedIn ⇒ Linkediin
  1. Encoding
    1. Obfuscate and evade detection
    1. Base64, URL encoding, HTML encoding: Encoding url before sending to bypass detection
    1. Obscure JavaScript.
  1. Attachments
    1. Download and execute : Sending malware in email attachment
  1. Abuse of Legitimate Services : Use Legitimate Services for sharing malware.
    1. Google Drive, Dropbox, etc.
    1. Using trusted reputation to send malware.

Email Analysis Methodology

  • Initial Triage
    • Quickly assess and prioritize.
  • Header and Sender Examination
    • Investigate MTAs, addresses, IPs, etc.
    • Identify the true origin and check authenticity
  • Content Examination
    • Analyze email content for language, formatting, etc.
    • Look for social engineering red flags.
  • Web and URL Examination
    • Collect web artifacts
    • Utilize tools to inspect URLs and domains.
  • Attachment Examination
    • Securely extract and analyze attachments.
    • Checking file reputation and sandboxing.
  • Contextual Examination
    • Consider broader context, recent or current incidents.
    • Look for patterns and assess scope.
  • Defense Measures
    • Take reactive defense actions (if needed)
    • Take proactive defense actions
    • Communicate with users and stakeholders
  • Documentation and Reporting
    • Maintain records of findings, verdicts, and actions taken through detailed reports.
    • Close out alerts and tickets.

Reactive Phishing Defense

  • Containment:
    • Determine scope (Find out how many received the phishing mail, and who received)
    • Quarantine (Quarantine the affected system or user)
    • Block sender artifacts (Block sender email, domain, sub domain)
    • Block web artifacts (Block malicious urls, keywords, message, attacker server addresses)
    • Block File artifacts (Block malicious attachments use name or hashes of files, executable files)
  • Eradication
    • Remove malicious emails (search and remove malicious emails)
      • Content search and eDiscovery (search email boxes of all)
    • Remove malicious files
    • Abuse form submissions (Reporting hosting sites about the malicious URLs).
    • Credential changes
    • Reimaging (Recover and restore systems image)
  • Recovery
    • Restore systems
  • Communication
    • Notify affected users.
  • User Education
    • End-user training

Proactive Phishing Defence

  • Email filtering (block suspcious email, URL before reaching, using rules, pattern matching, ML)
  • Marking external emails (waring users for potential risks from external senders)
  • URL Scanning and Blocking
    • Real-time URL inspection (can use a proxy server for routing urls for detecting, or using Endpoint email detection, Redirections)
    • Block recently registered domains (use Whois to find registration details)
  • Attachment Filtering
    • File extension blocks (like exe, vb.net, pdf, ps1, iso, etc)
    • Attachment sandboxing (Deploying automatic sandbox solutions)
  • Email Authentication Methods
    • SPF
    • DKIM
    • DMARC
  • User Training
    • Security awareness training
    • Phishing simulation exercises
    • Reporting Functionality
